How to design products that respect the mobile experience without forgetting that desktop is still where work happens.

## Where Your Users Actually Are

Mobile-first is the default advice, but the right answer depends on the product. A consumer social app is mobile-first. A B2B analytics dashboard often is not. Look at the data before committing.

## Design the Smallest Frame First

Designing the smallest frame first forces hard choices early. What is essential. What can be hidden. What can be cut. The discipline produces better products at every size.

## Then Expand With Intent

Bigger screens are not just smaller screens with more padding. Use the extra space to expose information and reduce navigation, not just to scale things up.

- Show more at once on desktop
- Reduce the number of clicks
- Reveal secondary actions
- Add power-user shortcuts

## Touch and Cursor Are Different

Hover states do not exist on touch. Click targets need to be bigger. Animations that feel snappy on a desktop can feel slow on a phone. Design for the input, not just the screen size.

## Test on Real Devices

Responsive previews in design tools lie. There is no substitute for opening the product on an actual phone and an actual laptop and using it like a normal person.
