The case for bringing in design before the product is built, not after the launch underwhelms.

## Design Is Strategy in Disguise

Treating design as a coat of paint applied at the end of a project is the most expensive mistake an early team can make. Design decisions made before the first line of code shape the product, the brand, and the team culture.

## Common Symptoms of Late Design

When design comes in too late, the symptoms are predictable.

- A product that works but feels generic
- An information architecture that nobody can explain
- Features that fight each other for attention
- A brand that looks fine but says nothing

## What a Design Partner Actually Does

A good design partner is not a vendor of pixels. They help you sharpen the value proposition, simplify the product, and build a brand that earns trust. The output is screens and assets. The value is in the thinking.

## When to Engage

The right time to bring in design is usually six to twelve months earlier than founders think. Even a short engagement at the start of a project can save months of rework later.

## Pick a Partner, Not a Vendor

Look for a team that pushes back, asks hard questions, and is willing to disagree with you. Vendors say yes. Partners help you make better decisions.
