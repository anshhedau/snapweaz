Why email design is harder than web design, and the principles that produce emails people actually read.

## Email Is Not the Web

The same techniques that work on a modern website often break in email. Email clients are inconsistent, conservative, and unforgiving. Designing for them requires a different mindset.

## Keep the Structure Simple

Single-column layouts work everywhere. Multi-column layouts work some places. Plain HTML with minimal CSS is the most reliable foundation. Resist the urge to be clever.

## The First Line Decides

Subject line and preview text are the entire ad for your email. If they do not earn the open, the rest of the design does not matter.

- A subject line of six to ten words
- Preview text that adds, not repeats
- A clear sender name
- Consistent send timing

## Design for Skim

Most readers will not read every word. Use clear headings, short paragraphs, and one obvious call to action. If the reader can get the gist in ten seconds, you have done the work.

## Test in Real Clients

Email previews lie. Test in actual Gmail, actual Outlook, and actual Apple Mail before sending. The differences are real and they will bite you.
