Conversion is not about dark patterns. It is about clarity, trust, and removing every reason to hesitate.

## Clarity Beats Cleverness

Most underperforming landing pages confuse the visitor in the first three seconds. A clear headline that names the audience and the outcome will beat a clever one almost every time. Read the headline out loud. If you would not say it to a person, do not put it on the page.

## The Five Questions Every Page Must Answer

Visitors silently ask the same questions. The faster the page answers them, the higher the conversion.

- What is this
- Who is it for
- Why should I care
- How does it work
- What happens if I click

## Trust Lives in the Details

Testimonials, logos, real photos of real people, and specific numbers all compound. A vague claim like 'trusted by hundreds' is weaker than a single named customer with a specific result.

## One Page, One Job

Landing pages with three competing calls to action convert worse than pages with one. Pick the single action you want a visitor to take and make every element of the page serve it.

## Test, But Test the Right Things

Button color is almost never the bottleneck. Headlines, offers, and audience targeting are. Run experiments on the things that move the number, not the things that are easy to change.
