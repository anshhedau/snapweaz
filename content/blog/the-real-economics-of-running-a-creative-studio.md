An honest look at how small studios make money, where the margin actually lives, and how to build a sustainable practice.

## Revenue Is Not Profit

Top-line agency revenue is one of the most misleading numbers in the industry. Many studios with impressive billings run on razor thin margins because their utilization, pricing, or scope discipline is broken.

## Three Levers That Actually Matter

Studio profitability comes down to three numbers. Average project size, utilization, and gross margin per project. Improve any one of them and the business gets healthier.

- Average project size
- Billable utilization across the team
- Gross margin per project
- Cost of acquiring each new client

## Price for the Outcome

Hourly pricing punishes you for being fast and experienced. Outcome-based or fixed-scope pricing rewards efficiency and aligns incentives. The shift is uncomfortable but transformative once you cross it.

## Protect the Roadmap

Every studio eventually feels the pull of taking any project that walks in the door. The healthiest studios say no to most of them. A clear focus on a specific kind of client makes the marketing, sales, and delivery dramatically easier.

## Build Recurring Revenue

Project work is lumpy. Retainers, support contracts, and productized services smooth the cash flow and reduce founder anxiety. Even a small recurring base changes how the business feels to run.
