When motion is used well, the product feels alive. When it is overused, it feels exhausting. Here is the line between them.

## Motion Has a Job

Every animation in a product should answer a question. Where did this come from. Where is it going. What just changed. Motion without a job becomes noise the brain learns to tune out.

## Time and Easing Are Everything

Most bad animations are too slow or use the wrong easing curve. Two hundred milliseconds with a natural ease covers ninety percent of UI transitions. Save the dramatic curves for marketing moments.

## Respect Reduced Motion Preferences

Users with vestibular sensitivities can become physically ill from heavy animation. The prefers-reduced-motion media query exists for a reason. Honor it everywhere, not just on a settings page.

- Disable parallax effects
- Replace big movement with cross-fades
- Keep meaningful state changes
- Test with the setting enabled

## Performance Comes First

Animations that drop frames feel worse than no animation at all. Stick to transform and opacity wherever possible, and profile on the slowest device you support.

## Marketing Motion Is Different

On a product page, dramatic animation can earn attention. Inside the product, the same animation feels exhausting after the third use. Design with the context in mind.
