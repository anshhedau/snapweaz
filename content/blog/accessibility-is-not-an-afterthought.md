Why accessibility belongs at the start of every design and engineering decision, and how to build it into your team's habits.

## Accessibility Is Quality

The frame of accessibility as a special concern for a small audience is outdated and harmful. Accessible products are better products for everyone. Captions help in noisy rooms. High contrast helps in sunlight. Keyboard navigation helps power users.

## Start With Semantic HTML

The simplest accessibility win is using the right element for the job. Buttons are buttons. Links are links. Headings are in order. Most accessibility bugs disappear when the markup is correct from the start.

## Color Contrast Is Not Optional

A surprising amount of premium-looking design fails basic contrast checks. Tools like the browser dev tools and online checkers will tell you the truth. Fix the issues before the user has to.

- 4.5:1 for body text
- 3:1 for large text
- 3:1 for interactive elements
- Do not rely on color alone to convey meaning

## Test With a Keyboard and a Screen Reader

Every designer and engineer on the team should know how to navigate the product with only a keyboard. A weekly thirty-minute session is enough to surface most issues before they ship.

## Make Accessibility a Shared Goal

When accessibility lives only with one specialist, it becomes a bottleneck. When it lives with everyone, it becomes a habit. Train the whole team and celebrate accessibility wins alongside feature launches.
