Design systems are powerful but expensive. Here is a framework for deciding when your team is ready and what to ship first.

## The Real Cost of a Design System

Teams underestimate the maintenance burden of a design system. Components need owners, documentation needs updating, and every release creates pressure on the consumers of the library. Before starting, decide who will care for it after launch.

## Signs You Are Ready

Not every product needs a system on day one. Wait until the pain is real and visible across the team.

- Multiple designers ship inconsistent UI weekly
- Engineers rebuild the same components on every project
- Onboarding new hires takes longer than it should
- Marketing and product visuals are drifting apart

## Start Small and Useful

The fastest path to adoption is shipping a tiny system that solves a real problem. Buttons, inputs, typography, color, and spacing are usually enough for a first release. Resist the urge to ship every component before anyone uses it.

## Use Tokens From the Beginning

Design tokens are the single most important investment in a modern system. They let you change themes, support dark mode, and rebrand without touching components. Even a system with five tokens is more durable than one with none.

## Document Like It Is a Product

Treat your documentation as the primary user interface of the system. If a developer cannot find a component in thirty seconds, they will build their own. Invest in search, examples, and code snippets that copy with one click.
