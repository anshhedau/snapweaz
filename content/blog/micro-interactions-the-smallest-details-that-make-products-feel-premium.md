Why the tiny moments in a product carry so much weight, and how to design them without overdoing it.

## Small Moments Carry Big Meaning

The way a button responds to a press, the way a toggle slides into place, the way a checkmark appears after a save. These are the moments where a product earns trust and feels expensive.

## Start From the State Change

Every micro-interaction is a state change made visible. Identify the change, then design the smallest possible animation that communicates it clearly.

## Restraint Is the Whole Point

Overusing micro-interactions makes the product feel chaotic. Pick the moments that matter and leave the rest alone. A few well-placed details beat a dozen forgettable ones.

- Confirming a destructive action
- Saving or syncing
- Loading new data
- Completing a multi-step task

## Sound and Haptics Where Appropriate

On mobile, a subtle haptic can communicate more than a hundred milliseconds of animation. On the web, sound is almost always a mistake. Know the context.

## Respect Reduced Motion

If a user has reduced motion turned on, replace the animation with a snap or a fade. The communication still happens. The motion just steps out of the way.
