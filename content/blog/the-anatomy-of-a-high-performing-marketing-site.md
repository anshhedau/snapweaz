What separates marketing sites that quietly convert from ones that win awards but lose customers.

## The Site Is a Salesperson

A marketing site that does not convert is just an expensive brochure. Every page should have a single job and a single next step, and the rest of the design should serve those goals.

## Pages That Earn Their Place

The right page count is the smallest one that answers every question a buyer has.

- Homepage with a single core message
- Product or service pages that go deep
- Proof and case studies
- Pricing if it applies
- Contact that is easy to find

## Speed Is a Conversion Tool

Every second of load time costs conversions. Image weight, font weight, and unused JavaScript are the usual culprits. A marketing site should hit Lighthouse performance scores in the high nineties.

## SEO Without Compromise

Search-friendly does not have to mean ugly. Semantic markup, useful metadata, fast pages, and real content earn rankings without making the design worse.

## Measurement That Actually Matters

Vanity metrics like total visits are mostly noise. Conversion rate by source, time to first action, and revenue per visitor tell the real story.
