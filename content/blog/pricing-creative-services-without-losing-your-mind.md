A simple framework for setting prices that are fair to the client, sustainable for the studio, and easy to defend.

## Cost Plus Margin Is a Trap

Pricing based on internal cost trains the client to evaluate you on inputs rather than outcomes. It also caps your earnings to the hours you work.

## Value-Based Pricing in Practice

Charging based on outcomes sounds intimidating until you do it once. The trick is to anchor the conversation early. Ask what the project is worth to the business if it succeeds, and price as a fraction of that value.

## Price for Three Outcomes

Offer three tiers that map to different levels of ambition. Most clients pick the middle, but the high tier raises the anchor and makes the middle look reasonable.

- A minimum viable engagement
- A recommended scope that covers most needs
- A premium tier with strategic depth

## Write Estimates in Outcomes, Not Hours

An estimate that lists 'twelve hours of UI design' invites comparison shopping. An estimate that says 'a redesigned signup flow expected to lift conversion by ten percent' is much harder to commoditize.

## Raise Prices Often, in Small Steps

Studios that never raise prices fall behind their costs. Small increases every few months are easier to absorb than a single dramatic jump.
